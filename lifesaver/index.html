THE PROBLEM

Why People Die During Emergencies

Most emergency deaths are preventable. The first few minutes after an emergency are critical — but panic, lack of knowledge, and no internet access can cost lives.

😰

Panic & Confusion

During emergencies, people freeze up. Without clear, step-by-step guidance, even trained individuals struggle to remember what to do.

📵

No Internet Access

In rural areas, remote locations, and disaster zones, mobile data is often unavailable. Most first aid apps require constant internet connectivity.

⏱️

The 5-Minute Golden Rule

Brain damage begins in 4–6 minutes without oxygen. For most emergencies, the first 5 minutes are the most critical for survival.

📚

Lack of Awareness

Most people have never received formal first aid training. When an emergency strikes, they rely entirely on instinct — which is often wrong.

💓 CPR Technique

Place interlocked hands on the center of the chest. Push down 5–6cm at 100–120 compressions per minute. Give 2 rescue breaths every 30 compressions.

😴 Recovery Position

Roll the unconscious but breathing person onto their side. Tilt the head back to open the airway. Bend the top knee for stability. Monitor breathing continuously.

🩹 Bandaging Technique

Start below the wound, anchor the bandage, and wrap in an upward spiral pattern. Each layer should overlap the previous by 50%. Secure with a clip or knot — not too tight.

WHY IT MATTERS

The Golden Period of Emergency Care

5 MINUTES

🔴 0–2 min: Brain injury begins
🟡 2–5 min: Reversible damage
🟢 5+ min: Permanent damage risk

Every Second Counts

Medical professionals call it the "Golden Hour" — the critical window after an emergency where proper first aid can mean the difference between life and death, full recovery and permanent disability.

For cardiac arrest, brain damage begins in just 4–6 minutes. For severe bleeding, a person can lose consciousness in as few as 3 minutes. For choking, permanent brain damage occurs in under 5 minutes.

LifeSaver puts life-saving knowledge directly in the hands of every person — without requiring internet access — so the golden minutes are used wisely.
